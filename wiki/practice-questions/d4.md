# Practice Questions — Domain 4: Guidelines for Responsible AI

> 10 exam-style questions covering Task Statements 4.1 (responsible AI, bias/variance, detection tools) and 4.2 (transparency & explainability).

### Q1. A company wants to prevent its Amazon Bedrock chatbot from producing hate speech, discussing restricted topics, and revealing customers' personally identifiable information (PII). Which feature best addresses these requirements?
- A. Amazon SageMaker Model Monitor
- B. Guardrails for Amazon Bedrock
- C. Amazon SageMaker Clarify
- D. Amazon Augmented AI (Amazon A2I)

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

Guardrails for Amazon Bedrock lets you configure denied topics, content filters (hate, violence, etc.), and PII redaction to enforce responsible-AI policies on generative outputs. Clarify and Model Monitor focus on bias/drift, and A2I adds human review.
</details>

### Q2. Which of the following are recognized features (dimensions) of responsible AI? (Choose TWO.)
- A. Latency
- B. Veracity
- C. Token pricing
- D. Fairness
- E. Provisioned throughput

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B, D**

The exam lists responsible-AI features as bias, fairness, inclusivity, robustness, safety, and veracity. Latency, token pricing, and throughput are cost/performance considerations, not responsible-AI dimensions.
</details>

### Q3. A data scientist notices a model achieves 99% accuracy on training data but only 62% on new, unseen data. What is the most likely problem?
- A. Underfitting (high bias)
- B. Overfitting (high variance)
- C. Insufficient model complexity
- D. Data drift in production

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

A large gap where training performance is high but test performance is poor indicates overfitting — the model memorized the training data (high variance) and fails to generalize. Underfitting would show poor performance on both sets.
</details>

### Q4. A team wants to detect whether an ML model produces disparate outcomes across demographic subgroups such as age and gender, both before training (on the dataset) and after training (on predictions). Which AWS service is purpose-built for this?
- A. Amazon SageMaker Clarify
- B. Amazon Comprehend
- C. AWS CloudTrail
- D. Amazon Rekognition

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: A**

SageMaker Clarify computes bias metrics on training data and on model predictions and provides feature-attribution explainability, making it the tool for detecting bias across subgroups. The others do not measure bias.
</details>

### Q5. A company is choosing a foundation model and wants to follow responsible practices regarding environmental impact and sustainability. Which approach best reflects this goal?
- A. Always select the largest available model for maximum accuracy
- B. Select a right-sized model and consider energy-efficient, sustainable options rather than defaulting to the largest model
- C. Retrain the model daily to keep it current
- D. Disable all monitoring to reduce compute costs

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

Responsible model selection includes environmental and sustainability considerations — choosing an appropriately sized, efficient model reduces energy consumption and carbon footprint. Bigger is not automatically better or more responsible.
</details>

### Q6. Which document helps provide transparency by recording a model's intended uses, training details, evaluation results, and limitations in a standardized format?
- A. Amazon SageMaker Model Cards

- D. IAM policy documents

### Q7. A bank must be able to explain to regulators exactly why each loan application was approved or denied. When choosing between a highly interpretable model and a more accurate but opaque (black-box) model, which statement best describes the tradeoff?
- A. Opaque models are always illegal to use
- B. Interpretable (transparent) models are easier to explain but may sacrifice some predictive performance, while opaque models can be more accurate but harder to explain
- C. Transparent models always outperform opaque models on accuracy
- D. There is no relationship between interpretability and performance

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

There is a well-known tradeoff between interpretability and performance: simpler transparent models are easier to explain (important for regulated decisions) but may be less accurate than complex opaque models. The bank should weigh explainability needs against performance.
</details>

### Q8. Which are legal or business risks specifically associated with generative AI that responsible-AI practices aim to mitigate? (Choose TWO.)
- A. Intellectual property infringement claims
- B. Higher provisioned throughput costs
- C. Loss of customer trust from biased or hallucinated outputs
- D. Increased vector database storage
- E. Slower model inference latency

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: A, C**

Legal/business risks of generative AI include IP infringement claims, biased model outputs, hallucinations, end-user risk, and loss of customer trust. The other options are cost or performance concerns, not responsible-AI legal risks.
</details>

### Q9. A media company uses a generative model to auto-generate content but wants a human to review and approve low-confidence outputs before they are published. Which AWS service adds this human-in-the-loop review workflow?
- A. Amazon SageMaker Clarify
- B. Guardrails for Amazon Bedrock
- C. Amazon Augmented AI (Amazon A2I)
- D. Amazon SageMaker Model Monitor

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: C**

Amazon A2I lets you build human-review workflows that route predictions (for example, low-confidence ones) to human reviewers before use. Guardrails filters content automatically but is not a human-review workflow, and Clarify/Model Monitor address bias and drift.
</details>

### Q10. An organization builds an image classifier but discovers it performs poorly for certain skin tones because those groups were underrepresented in training data. Which characteristics of a dataset would most directly help prevent this? (Choose TWO.)
- A. A larger number of model parameters
- B. Inclusivity and diversity of represented groups
- C. A balanced dataset across demographic subgroups
- D. Higher inference temperature
- E. Shorter maximum token length

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B, C**

Responsible datasets should be inclusive, diverse, curated, and balanced so all demographic groups are adequately represented, reducing biased outcomes. Model parameters, temperature, and token length do not address representation gaps in the data.
</details>

### Q11. A retail chatbot built on Amazon Bedrock keeps generating responses that jailbreak into off-topic political debates and occasionally repeats profane user input. The team wants a managed way to block denied topics and filter profanity on both prompts and responses without retraining. What should they use?
- A. Fine-tune the foundation model on a cleaner dataset
- B. Configure Guardrails for Amazon Bedrock with denied topics and word/content filters
- C. Enable Amazon SageMaker Model Monitor
- D. Add Amazon Macie to scan the conversation logs

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

Guardrails for Amazon Bedrock apply configurable denied topics, content filters, and word filters to both input prompts and model responses, and work without retraining. Fine-tuning is slow and costly, Model Monitor tracks drift, and Macie classifies stored data.
</details>

### Q12. A responsible-AI feature ensures a model continues to perform reliably and does not fail or degrade when it encounters noisy, adversarial, or unexpected inputs. Which feature does this describe?
- A. Veracity
- B. Inclusivity
- C. Robustness
- D. Fairness

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: C**

Robustness refers to a model's ability to maintain reliable performance under noisy, adversarial, or out-of-distribution inputs. Veracity concerns truthfulness, inclusivity concerns representation of all groups, and fairness concerns equitable outcomes across groups.
</details>

### Q13. A model shows poor accuracy on BOTH the training set and the test set, and adding more training epochs does not help. Which two actions most directly address this situation? (Choose TWO.)
- A. Increase model complexity (for example, use a more capable model or add features)
- B. Apply stronger regularization to reduce variance
- C. Reduce the amount of training data
- D. Train longer or add more relevant, informative features
- E. Remove features to make the model simpler

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: A, D**

Poor performance on both training and test data indicates underfitting (high bias) — the model is too simple to capture patterns. Increasing model complexity and adding informative features/training help; regularization and simplification would worsen underfitting.
</details>

After launch, monitoring shows the model's real-world predictions increasingly favor one gender over time, even though it was fair at launch. The team wants to automatically detect this drift in bias and data quality on the live endpoint and trigger alerts. Which service best fits?
- A. Amazon SageMaker Model Monitor

- D. Amazon Rekognition

SageMaker Model Monitor continuously monitors deployed endpoints for data quality, model quality, and bias drift, emitting alerts when metrics deviate. Clarify computes bias at a point in time, Model Cards document models, and Guardrails is for generative content, not endpoint drift monitoring.

### Q16. Scenario: A healthcare provider must select a foundation model for clinical note summarization. Leadership wants to act responsibly regarding sustainability and cost while still meeting accuracy needs. Which selection approach best reflects responsible practice?
- A. Choose the largest frontier model available regardless of task, to guarantee accuracy
- B. Evaluate right-sized models, favoring an energy-efficient model that meets the accuracy requirement for the specific task
- C. Train a brand-new model from scratch on-premises
- D. Pick the cheapest model even if it fails the accuracy requirement

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

Responsible model selection balances performance needs with environmental/sustainability and cost considerations — choosing the smallest efficient model that still meets requirements. Defaulting to the largest model or training from scratch wastes energy, and an inadequate model fails the use case.
</details>

### Q17. A company fine-tunes a generative model on text scraped from the open web and now worries about legal exposure. Which legal risks of generative AI are most relevant here? (Choose TWO.)
- A. Intellectual property infringement claims from using copyrighted training material
- B. Increased Amazon S3 storage bills
- C. Loss of customer trust if the model reproduces biased or hallucinated content

Training on scraped web data raises IP infringement risk, and biased or hallucinated outputs can erode customer trust — both are legal/business risks responsible-AI practices address. Storage costs, latency, and context length are technical/cost concerns, not legal risks.
</details>

### Q18. What is the key difference between transparency and explainability in AI systems?
- A. They are identical terms with no distinction
- B. Transparency is about openness into how a model works and is documented (data, design, licensing), while explainability is about understanding why a model produced a specific output
- C. Transparency applies only to open-source models and explainability only to closed models
- D. Explainability makes a model faster; transparency makes it cheaper

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

Transparency concerns how open and well-documented a model is (training data, design, licensing, model cards), while explainability concerns interpreting why a model made a particular prediction (for example, feature attributions). They are related but distinct.
</details>

### Q19. Which principle is central to human-centered design for explainable AI?
- A. Removing humans from the loop to maximize automation speed
- B. Presenting explanations and controls that let the affected people understand, trust, and appropriately question or override AI decisions
- C. Hiding model internals so users are not confused
- D. Optimizing solely for the highest accuracy metric regardless of user needs

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: B**

Human-centered design for explainable AI focuses on the people affected — providing understandable explanations, fostering appropriate trust, and enabling users to question or override decisions. It does not aim to remove humans or hide how the system behaves.
</details>

### Q20. A data science team wants both to measure feature importance (why a model made predictions) AND to detect pre-training bias in the dataset across subgroups such as age and gender. Which single AWS service provides both bias detection and feature-attribution explainability?
- A. Amazon SageMaker Model Monitor
- B. Amazon Augmented AI (Amazon A2I)
- C. Amazon SageMaker Clarify
- D. Amazon SageMaker Model Cards

<details><summary>Đáp án & giải thích</summary>

**Đáp án: C**

SageMaker Clarify detects bias in training data and predictions and provides feature-attribution explanations (for example, SHAP values). Model Monitor watches drift, A2I adds human review, and Model Cards document models but do not compute bias or attributions.
</details>
